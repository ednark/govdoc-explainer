    Summary of Government Standards Document (13 FAM 300)

    1. Cyber Security Awareness Training (PS800)

      • Annual online training required for all department computer users.
      • Must be completed before OpenNet Plus access is revoked.

    2. Counterintelligence and Insider Threat Training (EX250/EX251)

      • Annual online training for deterring and mitigating insider threats.
      • Required for all employees, contractors, and others with OpenNet accounts.

    3. Training for Classifiers and Users of National Security Information (PK400)

      • Annual training for employees and contractors with security clearances.
      • Failure to complete results in loss of OpenNet access.

    4. Records Management Training (PK217)

      • Annual training for all personnel with OpenNet access.
      • Essential for compliance with the Federal Records Act and NARA mandates.

    5. Protecting Personally Identifiable Information (PII) Training (PA318)

      • Biennial training required for all OpenNet users.
      • Ensures compliance with the Privacy Act of 1974 and other OMB directives.

    Certainly, here's a minimal list of actions from the perspective of a DevOps technician to ensure compliance with the provided Government Standards Document:

    1. Cyber Security Awareness Training (PS800):

      • Ensure all team members complete and pass the annual online Cyber Security Awareness course.
      • Track and document completion dates to prevent lapses in compliance.

    2. OpenNet Access Management:

      • Monitor OpenNet access and ensure revoked access if the team members fail to meet training requirements.
      • Reinstate OpenNet access only upon course completion and compliance verification.

    3. Counterintelligence and Insider Threat Training (EX250/EX251):

      • Verify that all team members undertake the required annual counterintelligence and insider threat training.
      • Maintain a log of training completion for internal monitoring purposes.

    4. Classified Information Handling Training (PK400):

      • Ensure team members with security clearances complete annual training on proper handling of classified national security information.
      • Suspend access rights for non-compliance and restore only after successful training verification.

    5. Records Management Training (PK217):

      • Confirm that all personnel complete annual Records Management training.
      • Keep records of training completion and manage compliance through a shared dashboard or tracking system.

    6. Protecting Personally Identifiable Information (PII) Training (PA318):

      • Verify biennial completion of Protecting Personally Identifiable Information training by all OpenNet users.
      • Enable access to PA318 training via the FSI OpenNet website and document completion status.

    7. Data Encryption:

      • Implement encryption for data-at-rest and data-in-transit to safeguard personally identifiable information and classified data.
      • Regularly audit encryption protocols to align with best practices.

    8. Access Control Measures:

      • Enforce strict role-based access controls (RBAC) to limit data access to authorized personnel only.
      • Regularly update and review access permissions to ensure compliance with security protocols.

    9. Incident Response Plan:

      • Develop and maintain an incident response plan to handle potential cyber threats and PII breaches.
      • Conduct regular drills to ensure the team is prepared for security incidents.

    10. Regular Security Audits:

      • Schedule periodic security audits and vulnerability assessments to identify and mitigate potential risks.
      • Document audit findings and rectifications to demonstrate compliance.

    By adhering to these steps, a DevOps technician can ensure that the infrastructure supporting the web applications meets the compliance requirements outlined in the government standards document.

    Minimal Actions for a Web Application Project to Show Compliance with Government Standards

    1. Cyber Security Awareness Training (PS800) Compliance:

      • Ensure all project team members (employees and contractors) complete the annual Cyber Security Awareness course (PS800) via the FSI OpenNet website.

    2. Counterintelligence and Insider Threat Training (EX250/EX251) Compliance:

      • Have team members complete the annual online Counterintelligence and Insider Threat Awareness training (EX250 for cleared personnel, EX251 for uncleared personnel).
      • Track attendance and completion using the FSI's Student Information System (SIS) or iPost database.

    3. National Security Information Training (PK400) Compliance:

      • Ensure team members with security clearances complete the annual training course PK400 for proper handling of classified material.
      • Provide completion certificates and ensure ongoing access to OpenNet.

    4. Records Management Training (PK217) Compliance:

      • Verify that all project personnel complete the annual Records Management training (PK217).
      • Use the FSI online dashboard to track training and address scenarios for remote or non-OpenNet accessing personnel.

    5. Protecting Personally Identifiable Information (PII) Training (PA318) Compliance:

      • Enforce the completion of the PA318 course, which is mandatory every two years for all OpenNet users.
      • Supervisors must ensure workforce members maintain updates on PII protection training.

    1. Create a Training Log:

      • Maintain a centralized log to track completion statuses and deadlines for all mandatory training requirements for project personnel.

    2. Periodic Reviews:

      • Conduct quarterly reviews to ensure compliance with all training mandates and update the status log accordingly.

    3. Client Guidance:

      • Guide clients on their responsibilities for ensuring their personnel complete the required training by deadlines.

    4. Coordination with HR and Security:

      • Liaise with Human Resources and the Security Office to verify and monitor the fulfillment of all mandatory training activities for team members.

    5. Documentation and Reports:

      • Compile and submit necessary compliance reports to the appropriate oversight bodies, ensuring all training requirements are satisfied.

    6. Communication and Reminders:

      • Send regular reminders to team members about upcoming training deadlines to ensure timely compliance.

    By adhering to this list, project managers can efficiently guide their teams to meet government standards requirements, ensuring that all mandated training is completed and properly documented.
