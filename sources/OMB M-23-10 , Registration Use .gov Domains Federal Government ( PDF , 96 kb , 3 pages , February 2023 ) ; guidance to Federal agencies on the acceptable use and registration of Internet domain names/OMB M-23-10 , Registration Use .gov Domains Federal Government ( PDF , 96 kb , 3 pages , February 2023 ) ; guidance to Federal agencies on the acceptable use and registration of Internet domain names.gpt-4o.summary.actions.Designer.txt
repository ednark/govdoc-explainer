1. **Use .gov Domain:** Ensure that the web application uses a .gov domain for all official communication and public-facing components.

2. **Memorable Domain Name:** Choose a domain name that is short, memorable, and clearly descriptive of the government organization or service offered.

3. **Consistent Branding:** Maintain consistent use of the .gov domain across all pages and services to enhance public trust and recognition.

4. **User-Centric Design:** Design the user interface and experience to meet the needs of diverse user segments, ensuring accessibility and ease of navigation.

5. **Clear Communication:** Provide clear and authoritative information, services, and communication through the web application.

6. **Review Domain Naming Conventions:** Check that the domain name complies with OMB's domain naming conventions and requirements.

7. **Regular Review:** Periodically review the domain and user interface for compliance with OMB policies and to ensure it continues to meet user needs effectively.

8. **Feedback Mechanism:** Implement and maintain a system for users to provide feedback on their experience, which can be used to make continuous improvements.

9. **Security Best Practices:** Integrate secure design principles to protect user data and maintain the integrity of the web application.

10. **Accessibility Compliance:** Ensure the web application meets accessibility standards (e.g., WCAG 2.1) to be usable by individuals of all abilities.
