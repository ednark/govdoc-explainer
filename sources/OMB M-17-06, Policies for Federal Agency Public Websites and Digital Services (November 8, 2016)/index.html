OMB M-17-06, Policies for Federal Agency Public Websites and Digital Services (November 8, 2016)

    Source Text



    Document Summary: Government Standards for Public Websites and Digital Services

    Primary Standards, Guidelines, and Requirements:

    1. Establish Integral Digital Governance

      • Agencies must have a digital governance plan.
      • Governance plans should be available on the agency's website.

    2. Use Analytics and User Feedback

      • All agencies must use GSA’s Digital Analytics Program (DAP).
      • Agencies should collect user feedback through methods like surveys and usability studies.

    3. Make Information Searchable and Discoverable

      • Websites must have a functional search feature.
      • Ensure content is indexable by commercial search engines.

    4. Provide Open Data Public Engagement

      • Agencies must provide accessible data listings and feedback mechanisms.
      • Open source documentation and APIs should be available publicly.

    5. Provide Access on Multiple Devices

      • New websites and major redesigns must have responsive designs for different devices.

    6. Protect Privacy

      • Agencies must have a public-facing Privacy Program Page.
      • Clear and accessible privacy policies must be on all digital services.

    7. Implement Security and Privacy Controls

      • Follow FISMA and NIST guidelines for securing information and IT systems.
      • Information systems should be continuously monitored for security.

    8. Use Secure Connections (HTTPS)

      • All public websites must use HTTPS for secure connections.

    9. Use Only Approved Domains

      • Use .gov or .mil domains for all official public websites.

    10. Comply with Third-Party Website and Application Requirements

      • Register third-party digital services with the U.S. Digital Registry.
      • Comply with privacy protection requirements when using third-party sites.

    11. Ensure Information Quality and Accuracy

      • Follow OMB's Information Quality Guidelines.
      • Be transparent about the quality of disseminated information.

    12. Ensure Accessibility for Individuals with Disabilities

      • Websites must comply with Section 508 and be accessible to individuals with disabilities.

    13. Records Management

      • Properly manage and preserve Federal records on websites and third-party sites.

    14. Use Plain Writing

      • Ensure content is clear, concise, and easily understandable.

    15. Provide Multilingual Content

      • Provide website content in multiple languages as needed.

    16. Ensure Access to Mandatory Content

      • Include mandatory links such as USA.gov, privacy policy, FOIA page, and others on principal websites.

    17. Transition to Internet Protocol Version 6 (IPv6)

      • Ensure public-facing servers and services use IPv6.

    18. Consistent Look and Feel

      • Use the U.S. Website Design Standards for a unified appearance across all agency websites.

    Key Focus Areas:

    • Security

      • Use HTTPS connections.
      • Follow FISMA and NIST guidelines.
      • Manage domains properly.

    • Accessibility

      • Comply with Section 508.
      • Ensure websites are accessible to those with disabilities.

    • User Experience

      • Implement responsive designs.
      • Utilize analytics and user feedback.
      • Ensure information is searchable and discoverable.

    • Compliance

      • Use plain writing and ensure multilingual content.
      • Follow records management regulations.
      • Include mandatory content and links.


    Purpose of the Government Standards Document: Ensure federal websites are effective, user-friendly, and accessible to everyone.

    • DevOps Technician: Set up and maintain secure, compliant infrastructure for federal websites with tools for analytics and feedback.

    Sure, here's a minimal list of actions specific to a DevOps technician for ensuring compliance with government standards for web applications:

    1. Deploy HTTPS: Ensure all public-facing websites and services use HTTPS (per OMB Memorandum M-15-13).
    2. Use Approved Domains: Verify and use approved .gov or .mil domains for official websites.
    3. Analytics Setup: Implement the GSA’s Digital Analytics Program (DAP) on all public-facing sites.
    4. Secure DNS: Comply with DNSSEC requirements to secure the domain name system.
    5. Privacy Policies: Facilitate the inclusion of privacy statements and policies on websites.
    6. Record Management: Ensure proper configuration for logging and record-keeping.
    7. Access Controls: Implement strong access controls and identity management in compliance with OMB Memorandum M-08-23.
    8. Responsive Design: Ensure websites use responsive design for accessibility on all devices.
    9. Disaster Recovery: Set up robust backup and disaster recovery mechanisms.
    10. Compliance Updates: Regularly review and update security controls following NIST and FISMA guidelines.
    11. Load Testing: Implement load balancing and perform regular load testing to ensure uptime and scalability.
    12. Monitoring: Set up continuous monitoring for performance, vulnerability scanning, and incident response.

    These steps will help ensure that the web application's infrastructure meets the relevant government standards.



    Certainly! Here is a brief list of actions a Human Centered Designer (Responsible for the User Experience and User Interface) should take to ensure compliance with the relevant government standards:

    1. Responsive Design: Ensure the website is fully responsive for optimal performance on all devices (Section 5A).

    2. Analytics & Feedback: Implement the GSA's Digital Analytics Program (DAP) tracking code and integrate user feedback mechanisms (Section 2A, 2D).

    3. Search Functionality: Include a search function on the public website and optimize for commercial search engines (Section 3A, 3B).

    4. Open Data Access: Implement a machine-readable Public Data Listing following the Project Open Data metadata schema (Section 4).

    5. Privacy Notices: Create and display privacy policies on all major entry points and on pages collecting PII (Section 6B).

    6. Plain Language: Ensure all content is written in clear, concise, and well-organized plain language (Section 14).

    7. Accessibility: Implement and test for compliance with Section 508 and EIT Accessibility Standards (Section 12).

    8. HTTPS Secure Access: Ensure all web services and websites are accessible through a secure connection (HTTPS) (Section 8).

    9. User Interface Consistency: Follow the U.S. Website Design Standards for a consistent look and feel across all digital services (Section 18).

    10. Multilingual Content: Provide access to critical content in various languages based on user needs and analytics (Section 15).

    By focusing on these specific actions, a designer can effectively demonstrate compliance with the outlined government standards relevant to their role.



    From a developer’s perspective, focusing on implementing backend business logic for a Drupal web application, here’s a minimal list of actions to show compliance with the relevant government standards:

    1. Digital Governance Plan: Ensure the website governance plan is publicly posted and regularly updated on the Drupal platform.

    2. Analytics: Integrate the General Services Administration’s (GSA) Digital Analytics Program (DAP) tracking code in all public-facing pages.

    3. Search Functionality: Implement a robust search feature using GSA’s search solution or ensure compatibility with commercial search engines.

    4. Open Data: Maintain a machine-readable and human-readable Public Data Listing and set up a public two-way feedback mechanism.

    5. Mobile Optimization: Employ responsive design to ensure the application functions well on both desktop and mobile devices.

    6. Privacy Notices: Include a central Privacy Program Page and necessary privacy policies on all relevant pages, ensuring personal identifiable information (PII) handling transparency.

    7. Information Security: Follow FISMA and OMB Circular A-130 requirements to ensure all data is protected by established security policies and guidelines.

    8. HTTPS: Ensure all connections to the website use HTTPS.

    9. Approved Domains: Ensure the website uses an approved .gov or .mil domain unless there is a justified exception.

    10. Third-party Services: Register third-party services such as social media accounts with the U.S. Digital Registry.

    11. Information Quality: Implement workflows to ensure that all disseminated information is accurate, reliable, and appropriately formatted for user understanding.

    12. Accessibility: Comply with Section 508 accessibility standards, ensuring all content is accessible to individuals with disabilities.

    13. Records Management: Implement features to manage and retrieve Federal records created or received on the website as per records management laws.

    14. Plain Writing: Ensure all content is in plain writing, clear, and concise to meet the Plain Writing Act’s requirements.

    15. Multilingual Content: Provide translations of essential content for users with limited English proficiency as required.

    16. Consistent Look and Feel: Apply consistent user interface components and visual styles following the U.S. Website Design Standards.



    Certainly! Here is a minimal list of actions a Project Manager can take to show compliance with the government standards for a web application project:

    1. Establish Digital Governance:

      • Create and publicly post a digital governance plan.

    2. Implement Analytics and Feedback:

      • Integrate GSA’s Digital Analytics Program (DAP) tracking code.
      • Collect user feedback via surveys and usability studies.

    3. Enable Search and Discoverability:

      • Implement an effective search function on the website.
      • Ensure content is indexable by commercial search engines.

    4. Open Data Compliance:

      • Publish a machine-readable Public Data Listing.
      • Provide a Data Publication Process and feedback mechanism.

    5. Ensure Multi-Device Access:

      • Use responsive design for new websites and major redesigns.
      • Prioritize modernization based on user feedback and analytics.

    6. Privacy Protections:

      • Maintain privacy policies and Privacy Program Page.
      • Provide Privacy Act statements or privacy notices on online data collection forms.

    7. Information Security:

      • Follow FISMA and NIST guidelines for securing information and systems.
      • Ensure domains comply with HTTPS requirements.

    8. Use Approved Domains:

      • Use only .gov or .mil domains for official public-facing websites.
      • Register non-governmental URLs and plan for migration to approved domains.

    9. Third-Party Website Compliance:

      • Register third-party services in the U.S. Digital Registry.
      • Ensure third-party services comply with privacy and terms of use requirements.

    10. Ensure Information Quality:

      • Adhere to Information Quality Guidelines and regularly review content.

    11. Accessibility:

      • Comply with Section 508 requirements.
      • Establish an accessibility statement and appoint a Section 508 Coordinator.

    12. Records Management:

      • Implement proper records management for all digital content.
      • Ensure compliance with Federal records management laws.

    13. Plain Writing:

      • Use plain writing for all public-facing content according to the Plain Writing Act.

    14. Multilingual Content:

      • Provide appropriate content access for individuals with limited English proficiency.

    15. Required Links:

      • Post mandatory links such as USA.gov, privacy policies, FOIA page, etc.

    16. IPv6 Compliance:

      • Upgrade to IPv6 for all public/external facing servers and services.

    17. Consistent Look and Feel:

      • Adopt the U.S. Website Design Standards for visual and interface consistency.

    This list serves as a guide for a Project Manager to ensure compliance with necessary government standards while developing and managing web applications.
