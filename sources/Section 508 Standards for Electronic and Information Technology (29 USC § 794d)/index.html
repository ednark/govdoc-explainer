Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 In 1998, Congress amended the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 to require federal agencies to make their electronic and information technology (EIT) accessible to people with disabilities. The law 29 U.S.C § 794 (d) applies to all federal agencies when they develop, procure, maintain, or use electronic and information technology. Under Section 508, agencies must give disabled employees and members of the public access to information comparable to the access available to others. The U.S. Access Board is responsible for developing Information and Communication Technology (ICT) accessibility standards to incorporate into regulations that govern federal procurement practices. On January 18, 2017, the Access Board issued a final rule that updated accessibility requirements covered by Section 508 and refreshed guidelines for telecommunications equipment subject to Section 255 of the Communications Act. The final rule went into effect on January 18, 2018. The rule updated and reorganized the Section 508 Standards and Section 255 Guidelines in response to market trends and innovations in technology. The refresh also harmonized these requirements with other guidelines and standards both in the U.S. and abroad, including standards issued by the European Commission, and with the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG 2.0), a globally recognized voluntary consensus standard for web content and ICT.

It is important to understand Section 508 in the context of other laws related to federal disability policy. In addition to Section 508, the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 (PDF) has the following related sections: Section 501 - Prohibits federal employers from discriminating against qualified individuals with disabilities. It requires each federal agency to adopt the goal of having 12% of its workforce be people with disabilities and 2% of its workforce be people with targeted disabilities. Section 503 - Prohibits employment discrimination based on disability by federal contractors or subcontractors. Section 504 (PDF) - Prohibits federal agencies, programs, or activities from discriminating and requires reasonable accommodation for qualified individuals with disabilities. Section 505 - Contains provisions governing remedies and attorneys' fees under Section 501. Related laws include: Section 255 of the Communications Act - Requires telecommunications products and services be accessible to people with disabilities. Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) - The first major legislative effort to secure an equal playing field for individuals with disabilities.

    Certainly! Here are the minimal actions a DevOps technician must take to ensure compliance with Section 508 standards for a web application:

    1. Ensure Infrastructure Compatibility:

      • Verify that the hosting environment supports accessible technologies and standards like WCAG 2.0.

    2. Automated Accessibility Testing:

      • Integrate automated accessibility testing tools (e.g., axe-core) into CI/CD pipelines to catch and resolve accessibility issues early.

    3. Logging and Monitoring:

      • Implement logging and monitoring focused on accessibility errors and compliance, track their resolution.

    4. Secure Communication Protocols:

      • Ensure secure and accessible communication protocols (e.g., HTTPS) are used and maintained.

    5. Redundancy and Reliability:

      • Design and maintain infrastructure redundancy to ensure consistent, reliable access for users with disabilities.

    6. Backup and Recovery:

      • Establish secure, automated backup and recovery procedures to maintain data integrity and availability.

    7. Performance Optimization:

      • Optimize server performance to support screen readers and other assistive technologies without lag or downtime.

    8. Data Privacy Compliance:

      • Implement data privacy measures compliant with relevant laws to protect user information, particularly sensitive data related to disability.

    9. Accessibility Statements:

      • Host and maintain an accessibility statement on the web application in line with OMB requirements.

    10. Regular Updates:

      • Schedule and perform regular updates to infrastructure and software to stay current with evolving accessibility standards.

    By following these actions, DevOps technicians can ensure their infrastructure supports the necessary accessibility requirements defined by Section 508 standards.



    For a Designer specializing in Human Centered Design to ensure compliance with government accessibility standards for a web application, follow this minimal list of actions:

    1. Implement ARIA Landmarks: Use ARIA roles (e.g., role="banner", role="navigation", role="main", role="contentinfo") to provide clear structure.

    2. Contrast Ratio: Ensure text and interactive elements have a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text.

    3. Keyboard Navigation: Ensure all interactive elements are accessible via keyboard, including focus states.

    4. Alt Text for Images: Provide descriptive alternative text for all images and non-text content.

    5. Forms Accessibility: Label all form elements properly and ensure forms can be navigated by keyboard and screen readers.

    6. Error Identification: Clearly identify errors in form submissions and provide text cues.

    7. Consistent Navigation: Keep navigation consistent throughout the application to assist with predictability.

    8. Resizable Text: Allow users to resize text up to 200% without loss of content or functionality.

    9. Media Accessibility: Provide captions for all video content and transcripts for audio content.

    10. Descriptive Links: Use meaningful text for hyperlinks that describe the purpose or destination (avoid "click here" links).

    11. Semantic HTML Elements: Use appropriate HTML5 elements (e.g., <header>, <footer>, <article>, <section>) to enhance the logical structure of content.

    12. Dynamic Content Updates: Ensure dynamic content updates (e.g., via JavaScript) can be detected by screen readers.

    By focusing on these specific actions, a Designer can help ensure the User Experience and User Interface of a web application comply with essential accessibility standards.



    Certainly! Here’s a minimal list of actions that a developer specializing in Drupal should take to show compliance with Section 508 standards:

    1. Familiarize with WCAG 2.0 Guidelines:

      • Review the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG 2.0) as they are harmonized with Section 508 Standards.

    2. Use Accessible Themes and Templates:

      • Select Drupal themes and templates that are already compliant with WCAG 2.0.

    3. Implement ARIA Landmarks:

      • Use Accessible Rich Internet Applications (ARIA) landmarks to define regions of the page (e.g., banners, navigation, main content).

    4. Ensure Keyboard Navigation:

      • Make sure all interactive elements (links, buttons, forms) can be navigated using the keyboard.

    5. Text Alternatives:

      • Provide text alternatives (alt attributes) for all non-text content like images, videos, and audio.

    6. Form Accessibility:

      • Use labels and fieldsets for form elements to ensure they are accessible to screen readers.

    7. Color Contrast:

      • Ensure sufficient color contrast between text and background to make content readable for users with visual impairments.

    8. Descriptive Link Text:

      • Use descriptive text for links, avoiding vague terms like “click here” or “read more”.

    9. Error Identification:

      • Make error messages clear and provide context-sensitive help for form errors.

    10. Focus Management:

      • Ensure logical tab order for page elements and proper focus management for dynamically loaded content.

    11. Captions and Transcripts:

      • Provide captions for video content and transcripts for audio content.

    12. Test with Assistive Technologies:

      • Regularly test the website with screen readers and other assistive technologies to ensure accessibility.

    13. Update and Maintenance:

      • Continuously update and maintain the website for compliance as new content is added or updated.

    Each of these steps can help ensure that your web application meets Section 508 standards and provides comparable access to users with disabilities.
