### Minimal Compliance Actions for DevOps Technician:

1. **User Access Control:**
   - Implement strict user authentication and authorization mechanisms.
   - Ensure all users accessing the system meet the "cleared U.S. citizen" requirement.

2. **Data Protection:**
   - Encrypt sensitive data at rest and in transit.
   - Regularly update and patch systems to mitigate vulnerabilities.

3. **Activity Monitoring:**
   - Establish user activity monitoring on all network domains.
   - Set up alerts for anomalous behavior, such as unusual access times or unauthorized data copying.

4. **Access Logging:**
   - Maintain detailed logs of access to classified information and sensitive systems.
   - Ensure logs are stored securely and have limited access.

5. **Incident Response:**
   - Implement procedure for detecting and mitigating insider threats.
   - Ensure that there are protocols for responding to unauthorized disclosures or network intrusions.

6. **Network Security:**
   - Utilize firewalls, intrusion detection/prevention systems (IDS/IPS), and secure network architectures.
   - Restrict network access to authorized devices and users only.

7. **Compliance Verification:**
   - Regularly review and audit access controls and security measures.
   - Report on compliance status and any security concerns to senior officials and security officers.

8. **Data Retention:**
   - Follow established protocols for the retention and protection of sensitive data.
   - Ensure data is disposed of securely in accordance with departmental policies.

9. **Training and Awareness:**
   - Provide mandatory insider threat awareness training to new users within 30 days of onboarding.
   - Ensure annual training renewals for all users with access to classified information.

10. **System Classification:**
    - Clearly classify and mark information according to its sensitivity level.
    - Ensure compliance with marking protocols as detailed in the relevant FAM chapters.

By focusing on these succinct action items, a DevOps technician can help ensure that the infrastructure supporting the web application complies with the required government standards for information security.
