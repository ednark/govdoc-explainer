Certainly! Here's a minimal list of actions a project manager must take to ensure compliance with the government standards for web applications:

1. **Data Collection Compliance**:
   - Ensure all data collection methods are documented and comply with privacy regulations.

2. **Data Privacy**:
   - Implement data privacy practices as per 5 FAM 460.
   - Conduct Privacy Impact Assessments (PIA).

3. **Data Storage and Security**:
   - Follow data security protocols outlined in 12 FAM 500.
   - Ensure proper data encryption and access controls.

4. **Transparency**:
   - Maintain clear documentation on data usage and decisions.

5. **Data Sharing Agreements**:
   - Facilitate written agreements for data sharing with third parties.

6. **Machine-Readable Data**:
   - Ensure new data is Open and Machine-Readable by default as per Executive Order 13642.

7. **Quality Assurance**:
   - Implement procedures for data quality and integrity as outlined in Department of State Information Quality Guidelines (2002).

8. **Compliance with E-Government Act**:
   - Use internet-based information technologies to enhance citizen access.

9. **Monitoring and Reporting**:
   - Set up a system for ongoing monitoring and reporting of data incidents, especially involving Personal Identifiable Information (PII).

10. **Ethics and Transparency in AI**:
    - If AI is used, ensure it follows Executive Order 13960 on trustworthy AI.

11. **Data Retention and Disposal**:
    - Follow policies for data retention and disposal practices.

12. **Training and Awareness**:
    - Provide regular training for employees on data governance and security.

By ensuring these actions are tracked and completed, you'll maintain compliance with the relevant government standards required for your web application projects.
