Sure, here are the minimal actions a Designer, specializing in Human Centered Design, must take to ensure compliance with government standards:

1. **Responsive Design:**
   - Implement responsive design for all new websites and major redesigns.
   - Ensure the site performs equally well on non-desktop devices such as mobile and tablets.

2. **User-Centric Design:**
   - Use analytics and user feedback to design around user needs.
   - Continually test the website to ensure user needs are addressed.

3. **Privacy Policies:**
   - Include clear, accessible, and plain language privacy policies on all web pages that collect PII.
   - Provide privacy notices for any online collections of information where feasible.

4. **Accessibility:**
   - Ensure compliance with Section 508 standards for accessibility.
   - Regularly test new functionalities for accessibility.

5. **Search Functionality:**
   - Include an easy-to-use search function on public websites.
   - Ensure content is indexable and searchable by commercial search engines.

6. **Consistency:**
   - Maintain a consistent look and feel across all web pages.
   - Adhere to the U.S. Website Design Standards for user interface components and visual styles.

7. **Multilingual Content:**
   - Provide appropriate access for people with limited English proficiency as required by DOJ guidance.

8. **Information Quality:**
   - Ensure the design facilitates presenting information in an accurate, clear, complete, and unbiased manner.
   - Clearly identify and differentiate between internally produced and externally linked content.

9. **Security:**
   - Ensure all publicly accessible websites and services use HTTPS for secure connections.

10. **User Feedback Mechanisms:**
    - Implement visible and accessible mechanisms for collecting user feedback on the data page.

These steps are aimed at ensuring that user experience and interface design comply with the specified federal government standards while enhancing usability and accessibility.
