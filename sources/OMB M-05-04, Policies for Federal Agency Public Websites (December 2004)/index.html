    Summary of Primary Standards, Guidelines, and Requirements

    1. Establish Integral Digital Governance

    • Develop a comprehensive digital services strategy.
    • Publicly post and update the governance plan.

    2. Use Analytics and User Feedback

    • Participate in the GSA Digital Analytics Program (DAP).
    • Use qualitative and quantitative data for user needs.
    • Use Fast Track PRA for feedback.

    3. Make Information Searchable and Discoverable

    • Implement a site-wide search function.
    • Ensure content is indexed by search engines.

    4. Provide Open Data Public Engagement

    • Provide machine-readable Public Data Listings.
    • Ensure ongoing data publication processes.
    • Offer public feedback mechanisms and developer APIs.

    5. Provide Access on Multiple Devices

    • Ensure responsive design for new sites and redesigns.
    • Use analytics for optimizing legacy sites for mobile.

    6. Protect Privacy

    • Maintain a Privacy Program Page.
    • Post Privacy Impact Assessments and Privacy Act Statements.

    7. Implement Information Security and Privacy Controls

    • Comply with FISMA and other relevant laws.
    • Follow NIST guidelines for security.

    8. Use Secure Connections (HTTPS)

    • Require HTTPS for all public-facing sites and services.

    9. Use Only Approved Domains

    • Operate websites on .gov or .mil domains.
    • List non-government URLs and migrate if necessary.

    10. Comply with Third-Party Website and Application Requirements

    • Register public-facing digital services with the U.S. Digital Registry.
    • Ensure third-party services comply with privacy and terms of service requirements.

    11. Ensure Information Quality and Accuracy

    • Adhere to Information Quality Act standards.
    • Clearly identify limitations and manage external links.

    12. Ensure Accessibility for Individuals with Disabilities

    • Comply with Section 508 standards.
    • Include accessibility statements and appoint a Section 508 Coordinator.

    13. Comply with Records Management

    • Manage Federal records on agency and third-party websites according to laws and policies.

    14. Use Plain Writing

    • Ensure web content is clear, concise, and well-organized.

    15. Provide Multilingual Content

    • Provide content in other languages as required by mission and user feedback.

    16. Ensure Access to Mandatory Content

    • Post mandatory links, including USA.gov and privacy policy.

    17. Transition to Internet Protocol Version 6 (IPv6)

    • Upgrade to IPv6 for public-facing servers and services.

    18. Ensure a Consistent Look and Feel Across Websites

    • Use the U.S. Website Design Standards for consistency.

    Certainly! Here is a minimal list of actions a DevOps technician must take to ensure compliance with the government standards for federal agency public websites and digital services:

    1. Secure HTTPS Implementation

      • Enforce HTTPS for all public-facing websites and services.
      • Ensure certificates are up-to-date and adhere to OMB Memorandum M-15-13.

    2. Domain Management

      • Use only approved .gov or .mil domains for official public-facing websites.
      • Register all third-party services with U.S. Digital Registry.

    3. Analytics Integration

      • Implement the General Service Administration’s (GSA) Digital Analytics Program (DAP) tracking code.

    4. Information Security

      • Follow FISMA and OMB Circular A-130 guidelines.
      • Implement NIST SP 800-44 and other relevant 800 series guidelines for securing public web servers.

    5. Section 508 Compliance

      • Ensure all electronic information technology adheres to the EIT Accessibility Standards.

    6. IPv6 Transition

      • Upgrade servers and services to support native IPv6.

    7. Public Data and API Access

      • Ensure that machine-readable Public Data Listings are accessible via www.[agency].gov/data.json.
      • Provide APIs on www.[agency].gov/developer.

    8. Privacy and Policy Pages

      • Maintain a central privacy program page as per requirement.
      • Ensure privacy policies are accessible on principal, sub-agency, component, and program websites.

    9. Search and SEO

      • Implement search functionality on public websites.
      • Ensure content is indexed by commercial search engines.

    10. Consistent Look and Feel

      • Apply U.S. Website Design Standards for consistent user experience across all federal websites.

    11. Records Management

      • Ensure proper management of federal records created and maintained on the websites or third-party platforms.

    By following these actions, a DevOps technician can help ensure that the web application meets the necessary government standards.

      • Ensure compliance with Section 508 standards for accessibility.
      • Regularly test new functionalities for accessibility.

    5. Search Functionality:

      • Include an easy-to-use search function on public websites.
      • Ensure content is indexable and searchable by commercial search engines.

    6. Consistency:

      • Maintain a consistent look and feel across all web pages.
      • Adhere to the U.S. Website Design Standards for user interface components and visual styles.

    7. Multilingual Content:

      • Provide appropriate access for people with limited English proficiency as required by DOJ guidance.

    8. Information Quality:

      • Ensure the design facilitates presenting information in an accurate, clear, complete, and unbiased manner.

    9. Security:

      • Ensure all publicly accessible websites and services use HTTPS for secure connections.

    10. User Feedback Mechanisms:

      • Implement visible and accessible mechanisms for collecting user feedback on the data page.
