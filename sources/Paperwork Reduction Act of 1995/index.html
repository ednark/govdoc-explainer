The Paperwork Reduction Act (PRA) of 1995 requires that agencies obtain Office of Management and Budget (OMB) approval before requesting most types of information from the public. “Information collections” include forms, interviews, and record keeping, to name a few categories.     Certainly! Here’s a minimal list of actions a DevOps technician must take to ensure compliance with the relevant government standards, specifically focusing on the Paperwork Reduction Act (PRA):

    1. Enable Secure Data Collection:

      • Implement secure forms and data entry points.
      • Ensure encryption of data in transit and at rest.

    2. Access Management:

      • Configure role-based access controls to restrict who can view and handle collected data.
      • Document access policies and keep records of who accesses sensitive data.

    3. Automate Audit Logs:

      • Enable logging for all data collection points and access events.
      • Ensure logs are tamper-proof and stored securely.

    4. Service Delivery Feedback Mechanism:

      • Implement a fast-track process to collect feedback on service delivery as per OMB M-11-26.
      • Use web-based interactive technologies for feedback while ensuring PRA compliance.

    5. Maintain Compliance Documentation:

      • Keep thorough documentation of compliance with the PRA, including obtained OMB approvals.
      • Regularly update compliance documentation as part of the deployment pipeline.

    6. Data Minimization Practices:

      • Configure the infrastructure to collect only the necessary data as specified by PRA requirements.
      • Periodically review and minimize data storage to what is essential.

    7. Regular Security Audits:

      • Conduct regular security audits to ensure compliance with government standards.
      • Implement tools for continuous monitoring and alerting of potential compliance breaches.

    These actions will help ensure that the web application infrastructure is compliant with the relevant government standards, particularly the PRA, from a DevOps perspective.



    For a web application to show compliance with the Government Standards related to the Paperwork Reduction Act (PRA) from the perspective of a Designer specializing in Human-Centered Design, the following actions are essential:

    1. Simplify User Forms: Design forms to collect only essential information needed for government approvals to minimize user burden.

    2. Obtain OMB Approval Reference: Ensure that any information collection from the public has an OMB approval number displayed on the form or interface.

    3. Provide Clear Instructions: Design interfaces with clear, concise instructions to help users understand the purpose of information collection and how to complete forms accurately.

    4. Include Privacy Notices: Incorporate privacy statements that inform users how their data will be used and protected, in line with PRA requirements.

    5. Facilitate Usability Testing: Conduct regular user testing sessions to gather feedback and ensure the application meets user needs without unnecessary complexity.

    6. Use Accessible Design Standards: Employ accessibility standards (like WCAG) to ensure all users, including those with disabilities, can access and use the application effectively.

    7. Solicit User Feedback: Build in mechanisms for users to easily provide feedback on form usability and overall experience, aligning with the fast-track feedback processes under the PRA.

    8. Inform About Data Collection: Clearly inform users when their data is being collected, why it is being collected, and how long it will be retained.

    By focusing on these actions, the Designer can ensure that the web application aligns with the standards and complies with the Paperwork Reduction Act from a user experience perspective.
